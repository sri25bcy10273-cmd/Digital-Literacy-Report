# Digital-Literacy-Report
The document follows a fictional second-year engineering student named Arjun through four real-world scenarios that expose a common gap in today's students: being digitally active without being digitally aware. It is structured across six parts:

The Illusion of Digital Competence – How familiarity with tools like Canva and Google Slides creates a false sense of digital skill, without deeper understanding of copyright, privacy, or credibility.
Digital Identity – Arjun's inflated and incomplete online profiles on LinkedIn, GitHub, and Kaggle highlight how students prioritize visibility over genuine competence.
Collaboration Without Learning – A group assignment completed efficiently using shared tools, where most members couldn't explain their own contributions, revealing how digital platforms can mask passive participation.
Professional Communication – Arjun's poorly written email to a faculty member illustrates how informal digital habits bleed into professional spaces, affecting first impressions.
Cyber Crime Awareness vs. Behavior – Despite knowing about phishing, Arjun falls for a scam, demonstrating that awareness alone does not guarantee safe behavior.
